Colour Contrast

Why is it so important?

When designing a website accessible colour contrast is often given a back seat to incorporate designs which appear more appealing.

So why should we worry about colour contrast if it would mean compromising aesthetics?

For a start, having appropriately contrasting colour combinations on your site means including people with colour vision deficiency.

Also, people with moderately low vision can have difficulty telling certain colours apart so the right level of contrast will help.



To this end WCAG have set out guidelines that outline the right level of contrast to be used.

WCAG Colour Contrast Standards

To meet the WCAG standards the following colour contrast ratios have to be at least:

  • 4.5:1 for normal text
  • 3:1 for large text
  • 3:1 for graphics and user interface components
